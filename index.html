Our mission

Why OpenMessaging ?

Messaging and Streaming products have been widely used in modern architecture and data processing, for decoupling, queuing, buffering, ordering, replicating, etc. But when data transfers across different messaging and streaming platforms, the compatibility problem arises, which always means much additional work. Although JMS was a good solution during the past decade. But it is limited in java environment, lacks specified guidelines for load balance/fault-tolerance, administration, security, and streaming feature, which make it not good at satisfying modern cloud-oriented messaging and streaming applications.

Cloud Oriented

Designed for robust implementation on clouds with scalability, flexibility, isolation, and security.

Multi Domains

Does not rely on or favors any business products and provides a large range of support including finance, e-commerce and IoT.

Platform Independent

Compatible with most platforms including Java, c/c++, go, .net, python, etc.

Streaming

Enables big data streaming and computation to meet the cutting-edge business demands.

Standard Benchmark

Facilitates standard benchmark to allow standard tests.

Wire Level Pluggable

MQTT, AMQP, Private protocols are implemented to guarantee a wider range of compatibility.

Universal

Multi Domains

OpenMessaging is vendor-neutral and language-independent, provides industry guidelines for areas of finance, e-commerce, IoT and big-data, and aimed to develop messaging and streaming applications across heterogeneous systems and platforms.

We have focused on Messaging and Streaming field for years, during which we explored Corba Notification, JMS and other specifications to try to solve our stickiest business. But unfortunately, we fell short so we have no other option but to create a new cloud-oriented specification.

- Von Gosling, Alibaba Senior Architect, Co-Creator of Apache RocketMQ

There are many different open source messaging solutions, including Apache ActiveMQ, Apache RocketMQ, Apache Pulsar, and Apache Kafka. The lack of an industry-wide, scalable messaging standard makes evaluating a suitable solution difficult.

- Sijie Guo, PMC Chair of Apache BookKeeper

In developing Pulsar at Yahoo, we have placed since the beginning a lot of care in defining a simple yet effective abstraction model for messaging applications. We strongly believe that it would be very beneficial for the messaging ecosystem to have a new API standard that developers can use as a target for their applications.

- Matteo Merli, Co-Creator of Apache Pulsar

We have accumulated certain experience on MQs and APIs unification, and are willing to join OpenMessaging to construct a common standard of APIs together with others. We sincerely believe that a unified and widely accepted API standard can benefit MQ technology and applications relied on it.

- Neil Qi, DIDI Architect
